# CISSP Practice Quiz Answers - Video 6: Compliance and Regulations

Here are the correct answers and detailed explanations for the Video 6 quiz.

---

### Question 1
**Correct Answer: B**

*   **Explanation**: Under the Federal Sentencing Guidelines of 1991, senior executives (CEO, officers) and board members hold the ultimate responsibility and accountability for information security. They cannot escape liability by claiming ignorance. If they fail to practice due care (e.g., ignoring security reports), they face higher penalties and fines.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because although the IT manager acted negligently, corporate liability lies with the executive officers.
    *   *C is incorrect* because the analysts performed their duty by flagging the issue.
    *   *D is incorrect* because executive officers are required to know the security status of their organization, and ignorance does not reduce their liability.

### Question 2
**Correct Answer: C**

*   **Explanation**: To maintain 'Safe Harbor' immunity under the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA), an Internet Service Provider (ISP) must promptly remove or disable access to the infringing material once they receive a valid copyright violation notice.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because ISPs are not required to actively monitor network traffic for copyright issues to qualify for safe harbor.
    *   *B is incorrect* because terminating users immediately without verification is not mandated for basic safe harbor.
    *   *D is incorrect* because ISPs are subject to copyright laws but have conditional immunity if they follow safe harbor rules.

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer: B**

*   **Explanation**: The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies to any organization that collects or processes personal data of EU residents, regardless of where the organization is headquartered (extraterritorial scope). The principle of Storage Limitation dictates that data should only be kept for as long as necessary.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because GDPR has extraterritorial reach.
    *   *C is incorrect* because companies serving EU residents must comply with GDPR regardless of their nationality.
    *   *D is incorrect* because the EU-US Privacy Shield was declared invalid by the European Court of Justice (Schrems II case) and is no longer an active transfer mechanism.

### Question 4
**Correct Answer: C**

*   **Explanation**: PCI DSS Requirement 3 mandates the protection of stored cardholder data, including both strong encryption and secure cryptographic key management. Storing decryption keys in cleartext on the same server containing the encrypted database completely violates key security rules.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because firewalls are covered under Requirement 1, which does not govern key management.
    *   *B is incorrect* because physical security is under Requirement 9, and the issue here is logical key storage.
    *   *D is incorrect* because authentication is covered in Requirement 8.

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer: B**

*   **Explanation**: The corporate laptop is company property. If the company has established an **Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)** signed by the employee and displays login warning banners stating that all actions on company devices are monitored and employees have no expectation of privacy, the company is legally protected from privacy claims.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because GLBA applies to financial institutions safeguarding client data, not an employee's personal banking on company time.
    *   *C is incorrect* because CALEA applies to telecommunication providers building wiretap capabilities for law enforcement, not internal employer networks.
    *   *D is incorrect* because ownership alone does not grant employers unlimited monitoring rights without notification. Proper policies (like the AUP) must be established first to eliminate the expectation of privacy.
