# CISSP Practice Quiz - Video 6: Compliance and Regulations

Test your understanding of compliance, laws, intellectual property, privacy regulations, and security standards. Apply your governance mindset!

### Question 1
An organization suffers a severe data breach because a known SQL injection vulnerability on its public website was exploited. Security analysts had flagged the vulnerability weeks prior, but the IT manager took no action, and the CEO was completely unaware. How is liability and responsibility assessed under the Federal Sentencing Guidelines?

A. The IT manager is solely responsible since they had direct knowledge of the vulnerability.  
B. The CEO and top executive management are held responsible and accountable, and the company faces increased liability due to a lack of due care/due diligence.  
C. The security analysts are responsible for failing to escalate the warning directly to the board of directors.  
D. The organization is exempt from liability because the CEO did not have direct knowledge of the vulnerability.

### Question 2
A movie studio identifies that a customer of a major Internet Service Provider (ISP) is hosting and sharing copyrighted films. The studio sends a formal notice to the ISP. What must the ISP do to maintain its Safe Harbor immunity under the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA)?

A. The ISP must proactively monitor the network traffic of all subscribers to prevent future uploads.  
B. The ISP must immediately terminate the internet subscription of the accused user without verification.  
C. The ISP must promptly remove or disable access to the infringing material once notified.  
D. The ISP has no obligation because ISPs are completely exempt from all copyright laws.

### Question 3
A US-based retail company processes transaction records and email addresses of customers residing in the European Union. Which of the following is correct regarding GDPR compliance for this company?

A. The company is exempt from GDPR because its headquarters and servers are located in the United States.  
B. The company must comply with GDPR, and it can only store the personal data for the minimum time necessary to fulfill the transactions (Storage Limitation).  
C. The company is only subject to US privacy laws such as GLBA and CCPA because it is a U.S. entity.  
D. The company can transfer the customer data to the US freely because the EU-US Privacy Shield remains the active legal transfer mechanism.

### Question 4
A database administrator is configuring a new retail payment system. The administrator proposes storing credit card numbers in a database table with custom decryption keys saved in a plain-text configuration file on the same application server. Which PCI DSS requirement does this primarily violate?

A. Requirement to use firewalls to isolate default systems.  
B. Requirement to restrict physical access to cardholder data.  
C. Requirement to protect stored cardholder data with strong encryption and secure key management.  
D. Requirement to verify identities using multi-factor authentication tokens.

### Question 5
An employee uses a corporate-owned laptop to access their personal bank account. The company's security team uses a full packet inspection tool that decrypts SSL/TLS traffic, which records the employee's bank login credentials. The employee threatens to sue the company for invasion of privacy. What determines the legal outcome?

A. The employee will win because personal financial details are protected under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA).  
B. The company will win if they have a signed Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) and login banners stating that employees have no expectation of privacy on company equipment.  
C. The employee will win because full packet decryption is illegal under the CALEA wiretap law.  
D. The company will win automatically because they own the physical laptop.
