# CISSP Practice Quiz - Video 4: Social Engineering

Test your understanding of social engineering principles and methods. Apply your governance mindset!

---

### Question 1
A security engineer is performing a physical security audit of the head office. They observe an individual wearing a delivery uniform carrying two large boxes. As an employee approaches the locked badge-access door, the delivery person asks them to "hold the door for just a second." The employee complies and holds the door open, allowing the delivery person to enter. Which social engineering technique and human factor were exploited here?

A. Tailgating; authority was exploited.  
B. Piggybacking; human helpfulness was exploited.  
C. Tailgating; consensus was exploited.  
D. Piggybacking; scarcity was exploited.  

---

### Question 2
An attacker registers several domain names such as "micros0ft.com" and "mircosoft.com" with the intention of hosting credential-harvesting pages. Many users accidentally navigate to these sites due to typing errors on mobile touchscreens. What type of social engineering attack is this?

A. Pharming  
B. Typosquatting  
C. Watering Hole  
D. DNS Spoofing  

---

### Question 3
During a routine audit of the corporate trash containers located in an unsecured parking area, a security officer discovers several printed spreadsheets detailing internal system names, IP addresses, and email drafts. What attack does this practice expose the company to, and how is it best mitigated?

A. Shoulder surfing; mitigated by privacy screens.  
B. Hoaxes; mitigated by updating spam filters.  
C. Dumpster diving; mitigated by requiring secure shredding bins and paper shredders.  
D. Watering hole; mitigated by disabling external links.  

---

### Question 4
An organization experiences a security breach where several users on the finance team typed their banking URLs correctly but were silently redirected to a clone website that captured their login credentials. Investigations revealed that the users' local computers had a modified "hosts" file. Which of the following best describes this attack?

A. Watering Hole Attack  
B. Phishing  
C. Pharming  
D. Spear Phishing  

---

### Question 5
An employee receives an urgent email that appears to come from their company's CISO. The email states: "A critical vulnerability has been detected on your workstation. You must immediately click this link to run the diagnostic tool, or your system access will be terminated within 2 hours." The employee clicks the link and downloads malware. What combination of social engineering principles did the attacker exploit?

A. Social Proof and Consensus  
B. Likability and Scarcity  
C. Authority and Scarcity/False Urgency  
D. Familiarity and Consensus
