# CISSP Practice Quiz - Video 5: Keeping the Business Running

Test your understanding of Business Continuity Planning (BCP) and Disaster Recovery Planning (DRP) principles. Apply your governance mindset!

---

### Question 1
An organization experiences a major electrical fire at its primary headquarters. The IT team immediately begins restoring database backups and setting up the secondary disaster recovery site, while the customer service team initiates manual routing of customer calls to representatives' homes to ensure client services are uninterrupted. How should these activities be classified?

A. Both are disaster recovery planning (DRP) activities.  
B. Both are business continuity planning (BCP) activities.  
C. Restoring backups and setting up the secondary site are DRP activities; manual phone routing is a BCP activity.  
D. Restoring backups and setting up the secondary site are BCP activities; manual phone routing is a DRP activity.

### Question 2
An online retail organization determines that its e-commerce payment checkout system can be offline for at most 4 hours before the business suffers catastrophic financial loss. The IT systems team estimates it takes 2 hours to provision new server instances, and the database administration team requires another 1.5 hours to configure data feeds, restore transactional logs, and verify checkout operations. What are the correct time-based metrics for this process?

A. MTD is 2 hours, RTO is 1.5 hours, and WRT is 3.5 hours.  
B. MTD is 4 hours, RTO is 2 hours, and WRT is 1.5 hours.  
C. MTD is 3.5 hours, RTO is 2 hours, and WRT is 2 hours.  
D. MTD is 4 hours, RTO is 3.5 hours, and WRT is 0.5 hours.

### Question 3
A risk manager is calculating the threat of a severe flood affecting a corporate warehouse. The warehouse and its inventory have a total asset value (AV) of $10,000,000. Actuarial data suggests a flood of this magnitude occurs once every 50 years in this area, and would damage approximately 40% of the warehouse. What are the Single Loss Expectancy (SLE) and Annual Loss Expectancy (ALE) for this risk?

A. SLE = $4,000,000; ALE = $80,000  
B. SLE = $10,000,000; ALE = $200,000  
C. SLE = $4,000,000; ALE = $200,000  
D. SLE = $400,000; ALE = $8,000

### Question 5

A. Approve the engineers' request, as saving the company's core data is vital for survival.  
B. Allow only the most senior engineer to stay behind to reduce the number of exposed personnel.  
C. Order all employees to evacuate the facility immediately in accordance with the evacuation order.  
D. Request a special exemption permit from local law enforcement to allow the technicians to stay.
