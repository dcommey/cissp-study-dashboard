# CISSP Practice Quiz Answers - Video 1: Introduction

Here are the correct answers and explanations for the Video 1 quiz.

---

### Question 1
**Correct Answer: B**

*   **Explanation**: In CISSP, policies drive technical configurations. The administrator should follow the chain of command and governance processes. Writing a draft policy and presenting it to the manager aligns with a structured, governance-first approach. 
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because making direct changes without authorization or policy updates bypasses change management and governance (technician mindset).
    *   *C is incorrect* because it bypasses the manager and chain of command.
    *   *D is incorrect* because waiting for an annual cycle when an active vulnerability or gap is identified is passive and does not manage risk proactively.

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer: C**

*   **Explanation**: The CISSP covers 8 distinct security domains. The exam evaluates your understanding of security principles at a high level and how they align with business operations, rather than deep implementation details of any single technology.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A and D are incorrect* because the CISSP is highly theoretical and does not test command-line configurations or implementation details.
    *   *B is incorrect* because candidates cannot specialize; they must pass all domains.

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer: B**

*   **Explanation**: The CISSP is aimed at management, risk advisory, policy design, and security architecture. It prepares professionals to speak with authority on security architecture and management, rather than configure specific vendor products.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because hands-on skills can be tested in performance-based questions, but CISSP chooses not to focus on them.
    *   *C is incorrect* because theoretical frameworks and standards also evolve.
    *   *D is incorrect* because security is everyone's responsibility, but the CISSP specifically targets the design and oversight roles.

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer: B**

*   **Explanation**: The instructor stresses that CISSP places very little pressure on practical implementation skills (such as network scans or access lists). Instead, it entitles you to have an informed opinion on virtually any security-related discussion and to contribute meaningfully to design, policy, and architectural decisions.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A and C are incorrect* because CISSP is intentionally not focused on hands-on implementation or replacing operations staff.
    *   *D is incorrect* because the instructor explicitly adds the caveat "at least for a while, because things change and evolve" — no certification freezes you from being surprised forever.

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer: C**

*   **Explanation**: CISSP provides a broad, durable foundation, but the instructor emphasizes that things change and evolve. Professionals must continue learning to keep pace with new technologies and threats — a principle reflected in (ISC)²'s requirement to earn Continuing Professional Education (CPE) credits to maintain certification.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A and B are incorrect* because they assume security knowledge is static, which contradicts the instructor's point.
    *   *D is incorrect* because certification is maintained through CPE credits and annual maintenance fees, not by retaking the full exam yearly.
