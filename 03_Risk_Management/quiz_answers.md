### Question 1
**Correct Answer: B**

*   **Explanation**: 
    1. First, find the Single Loss Expectancy (SLE):  
       $$\text{SLE} = \text{Asset Value (AV)} \times \text{Exposure Factor (EF)}$$
       $$\text{SLE} = \$100,000 \times 0.30 = \$30,000$$
    2. Next, calculate the Annualized Loss Expectancy (ALE):  
       $$\text{ALE} = \text{SLE} \times \text{Annualized Rate of Occurrence (ARO)}$$
       *   An outage occurs once every 2 years, so the ARO is $0.5$ times per year.
       $$\text{ALE} = \$30,000 \times 0.5 = \$15,000$$
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because $30,000 is the SLE, not the annualized loss.
    *   *C and D are incorrect* because they result from incorrect multiplication or division.

### Question 2
**Correct Answer: C**

*   **Explanation**: The Delphi technique is designed to obtain consensus among experts through an anonymous, iterative feedback process. The anonymity ensures that dominant personalities, executive pressure, or social biases do not influence individual responses.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because public brainstorming workshops can be dominated by senior managers or directors.
    *   *B is incorrect* because structured interviews are labor-intensive and do not provide an anonymous mechanism to build consensus.
    *   *D is incorrect* because quantitative financial analysis uses formulas and numbers, not qualitative opinions from a diverse group of stakeholders.

### Question 3
**Correct Answer: B**

*   **Explanation**: Outsourcing email operations to a third party transfers the operational and administrative risk of email hosting. Purchasing insurance transfers the financial risk of a potential breach. Both are strategies of Risk Transference.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because neither action represents Acceptance (retaining risk) or Mitigation (installing local controls).
    *   *C and D are incorrect* because Risk Avoidance requires stopping the activity altogether (e.g., eliminating email completely). Since the company is still using email (via SaaS), they have not avoided the risk.

### Question 4
**Correct Answer: A**

*   **Explanation**: The Intrusion Detection System (IDS) is an IT software/hardware resource, which classifies it as a Technical (or Logical) control type. It functions to monitor, log, and alert on traffic (identifying incidents after they occur) without stopping them, making it a Detective control function.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *B is incorrect* because a preventative technical control would be an Intrusion Prevention System (IPS) or firewall that blocks the traffic.
    *   *C is incorrect* because the IDS is technical, not administrative (policies, training).
    *   *D is incorrect* because a physical control involves barriers, locks, or guards in the physical world.

### Question 5
**Correct Answer: C**

*   **Explanation**: Residual risk is the risk left over after security controls are applied. The starting level is Total Risk, and the effectiveness of the security controls is the Control Gap. Therefore, the formula is: Residual Risk = Total Risk - Control Gap.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A, B, and D are incorrect* because they represent mathematically incorrect arrangements of the risk factors.
