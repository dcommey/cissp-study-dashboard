# CISSP Practice Quiz Answers - Video 2: Security 101

Here are the correct answers and detailed explanations for the Video 2 quiz.

---

### Question 1
**Correct Answer: B**

*   **Explanation**: Due diligence is the research and planning phase (e.g., conducting assessments, documenting structures), and due care is the implementation and action phase (e.g., patching, monitoring). Because the security team proactively did both, the organization can demonstrate that they took reasonable steps to protect their assets. A zero-day exploit represents an unpreventable risk if best practices are followed.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because due care does not guarantee 100% prevention of breaches; it establishes a standard of reasonable care.
    *   *C is incorrect* because technical staff are rarely held personally liable; senior management holds the ultimate liability.
    *   *D is incorrect* because regulations do not generally exempt specific exploit types; compliance is assessed based on whether reasonable controls were active.

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer: C**

*   **Explanation**: Baselines establish the minimum security settings and configurations that all systems must meet. Blocking all inbound traffic by default on all newly spun-up servers is a classic example of a baseline configuration.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because standards are high-level, mandatory rules or technological choices (e.g., 'all databases must use AES-256').
    *   *B is incorrect* because guidelines are optional and advisory.
    *   *D is incorrect* because a procedure provides step-by-step technical instructions on how to install or configure the firewall.

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer: B**

*   **Explanation**: Repudiation is the threat that a subject will perform an action and then deny it. The goal of non-repudiation is to make it impossible for a user to deny their actions, which is achieved through digital signatures, hashing, and audit trails.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because spoofing targets authenticity (impersonating a user).
    *   *C is incorrect* because information disclosure targets confidentiality.
    *   *D is incorrect* because elevation of privilege targets authorization.

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer: C**

*   **Explanation**: Senior management is ultimately responsible and liable for security breaches. Therefore, they must review and sign off on all security policies. Delegating policy creation and signing to developers violates governance because the people who write the policy do not bear the ultimate accountability.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because developers can be technically competent, but the issue is governance and liability, not technical skill.
    *   *B is incorrect* because security frameworks (like NIST or ISO) are widely open and meant to be referenced by anyone.
    *   *D is incorrect* because internal teams write policies; auditors verify compliance with those policies.

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer: C**

*   **Explanation**: Authorization takes an authenticated subject and grants them specific access levels (permissions) to a passive object.
*   **Why others are incorrect**:
    *   *A is incorrect* because identification is merely claiming who you are.
    *   *B is incorrect* because authentication is proving your claim.
    *   *D is incorrect* because accounting is keeping logs and tracking actions.
