# Quiz Answers & Explanations: Investigations and Ethics

## Question 1
**Correct Answer: B**

### Explanation:
* **A) Incorrect:** The Fagan Inspection is a highly formal peer-review software engineering model containing six stages, not an e-discovery process.
* **B) Correct:** The Electronic Discovery Reference Model (EDRM) defines standard stages for identifying, preserving, collecting, processing, reviewing, analyzing, producing, and presenting Electronically Stored Information (ESI).
* **C) Incorrect:** Common Criteria (ISO 15408) is a framework used to evaluate and certify the security functions of IT hardware and software products.
* **D) Incorrect:** The MITRE ATT&CK Matrix is a database of threat actor techniques and tactics, not a legal e-discovery model.

## Question 2
**Correct Answer: B**

### Explanation:
* **A) Incorrect:** An SDN tap is used to duplicate virtualized network traffic and has no functionality for protecting physical storage media.
* **B) Correct:** A write blocker intercepts and blocks any commands sent from a forensic workstation that attempt to write or modify data on the suspect's media (e.g., SSD or hard drive). This ensures that the original evidence remains completely unmodified and admissible in court.
* **C) Incorrect:** Syslog-ng is a network-based log aggregation protocol, not a disk-write prevention control.
* **D) Incorrect:** In-memory dumping software captures the contents of volatile RAM but does not block writes on storage disks; in fact, running the tool itself writes commands to RAM.

## Question 3
**Correct Answer: B**

### Explanation:
* **A) Incorrect:** The best evidence rule states that the court prefers the original document (rather than a copy or transcript), but it does not address hearsay objections.
* **B) Correct:** The business records exception allows logs to be admitted as evidence despite hearsay objections if they are recorded at the time of the event by a system with direct knowledge, maintained as a part of regular business activities, and collecting them is standard corporate practice.
* **C) Incorrect:** The parole evidence rule prevents parties in a contract dispute from introducing verbal agreements that contradict a final written contract.
* **D) Incorrect:** The plain view doctrine allows law enforcement to seize evidence that is visible without a warrant; it does not govern log hearsay rules.

## Question 4
**Correct Answer: C**

### Explanation:
* **A) Incorrect:** Thrill attacks are conducted by script kiddies seeking excitement or peer prestige; they do not target specific organizations for massive revenge-oriented data destruction.
* **B) Incorrect:** Business attacks (espionage) seek competitor trade secrets for commercial advantage, rather than complete destruction of the target's assets.
* **C) Correct:** Grudge attacks are motivated by a desire for revenge, often conducted by disgruntled employees. A script designed to wipe data if an admin's account is disabled is a logic bomb, representing a classic insider threat.
* **D) Incorrect:** Military attacks target national security data, and APT groups seek persistent, silent access to spy or exfiltrate data, rather than loud, destructive acts that instantly alert the victim.

## Question 5
**Correct Answer: B**

### Explanation:
* **A) Incorrect:** Canon 3 requires the consultant to provide competent service to employers/clients, but this canon is subordinate to Canons 1 & 2.
* **B) Correct:** Canon 1 (protect society, the common good, public trust, and infrastructure) is the highest priority in the (ISC)² Code of Ethics. Protecting the public trust and data privacy of thousands of customers outranks the client's financial/venture capital round interests.
* **C) Incorrect:** Canon 4 requires protecting the profession, which includes keeping skills updated; writing an exploit to prove a point is not required by Canon 4.
* **D) Incorrect:** Canon 2 requires acting honorably and legally, but the first priority remains protecting society (Canon 1). The consultant does not have a duty to file a lawsuit, but rather to ensure the threat to the public is addressed ethically.
